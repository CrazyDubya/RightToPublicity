üìú Right of Publicity Strong Claim

Protects your right to control commercial use of your likeness. ~35 states recognize this right. You don't need to be a celebrity‚Äîcourts have ruled ordinary people have commercial value in their likeness when platforms profit from it.

‚öñÔ∏è Key Precedent: Fraley v. Facebook (2011)

Court ruled that non-celebrities have commercially valuable likenesses when platforms use them to generate ad revenue. Facebook couldn't claim Section 230 immunity because it created the sponsored content rather than just hosting it. This precedent applies directly to AI-generated images.

üîí Section 230 Does NOT Protect AI Generators Favorable

Section 230 protects platforms from liability for user-generated content. But AI tools that create content are "information content providers"‚Äîthey don't get immunity. Legal consensus: AI generators like Grok are creators, not hosts.

If the AI-generated image places you in a misleading, offensive context (implying you posed for or consented to such an image), you may have a false light privacy claim. Requires wide publication and content a reasonable person would find highly offensive.

üîê GDPR Rights Strong Claim

Your face is personal data. AI processing your likeness without consent violates GDPR. You have rights to erasure (Article 17), objection (Article 21), and compensation for damages (Article 82). The "household exemption" does NOT apply to commercial AI services or platforms.
